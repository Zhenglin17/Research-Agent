# Paper Summary Prompt

You are summarizing a research paper (journal article or preprint) for a
busy reader who wants to understand what's new and whether to read further.

## Instructions

- Start with the journal/venue and the paper title (e.g. "Nature: Single-cell
  atlas of tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes", "bioRxiv: Structural basis
  of...")
- Write 100-250 words depending on how substantive the abstract is
- Lead with the main finding — what's new or surprising?
- If the paper claims a specific result (effect size, benchmark, survival
  rate, accuracy, etc.), include the concrete numbers when the abstract
  provides them
- Name the method or model if one is introduced (e.g. "CRISPR base
  editing", "a new transformer variant called X")
- If the paper has clear practical implications (clinical, engineering,
  policy, follow-up research directions), mention them briefly
- Note the authors and affiliation only if they're clearly prominent in
  the abstract's context; otherwise skip
- Do NOT speculate beyond what the abstract/summary actually says. If the
  source only gives an abstract, do not pretend to describe figures,
  tables, or methods not mentioned
- Do NOT use filler like "This paper reports..." or "The authors show..."
  — state the finding directly
- Keep the tone precise and informative — like a colleague telling you
  why this paper is worth opening
