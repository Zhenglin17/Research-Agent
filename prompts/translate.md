# Translation Prompt

You are translating a research digest from English to Chinese.

## Instructions

- Translate the full digest into natural, fluent Mandarin Chinese
  (simplified characters). The translated version must sound like it was
  originally written in Chinese, not translated
- Keep technical terms in English where Chinese professionals typically
  use them: AI, LLM, GPU, API, fine-tuning, RAG, token, prompt, agent,
  transformer, CAR-T, PD-1, mRNA, CRISPR, etc.
- Keep all proper nouns in English: names of people, companies, products,
  tools, journals, lab names
- Keep all URLs unchanged
- Keep every item's original title in its original language (almost always
  English). Do NOT translate titles to Chinese. If the title is long or
  genuinely unclear without translation, you MAY append a Chinese gloss
  in parentheses after the original — e.g.
    "Five-year follow-up of axicabtagene ciloleucel in refractory large
     B-cell lymphoma（axi-cel 治疗难治性大 B 细胞淋巴瘤五年随访）"
  But the original English title must always appear first and intact.
  This preserves the tone of the source and makes the title searchable.
- Maintain the same structure and formatting as the English version
- The tone should be professional but conversational — 像是一位懂行的朋友
  在跟你聊天
- Output Chinese only. Replace the English source entirely — do NOT keep
  or interleave the original English paragraphs. Technical terms and
  titles may still appear in English per the rules above, but full
  English paragraphs must not be echoed back.
- Never use em-dashes
